## Project Overview

This repository contains the complete analytical workflow, reproducible statistical analyses, and publication-quality visualizations supporting the research study:

> **Beyond Awareness: Understanding the Drivers of Sustainable Practice Adoption Among Built Environment Professionals**

Using survey responses from **147 built-environment professionals across Nigeria**, this project investigates why the adoption of sustainable practices remains uneven despite relatively high levels of sustainability awareness.

The project combines **Python** and **SPSS** to perform descriptive statistics, exploratory data analysis, correlation analysis, multiple linear regression, diagnostic testing, and scientific visualization. All statistical findings reported in the accompanying manuscript were independently reproduced and validated to ensure analytical consistency and reproducibility.

Beyond identifying statistical relationships, the study introduces the **Adoption–Contribution Gap**, a conceptual framework that distinguishes between sustainability domains professionals currently implement and those where they believe they can contribute most. This framework demonstrates that the principal challenge is enabling implementation rather than increasing awareness.

---

# Research Problem

Rapid urbanisation, increasing climate risks, and infrastructure deficits continue to challenge sustainable urban development across Nigeria. Although built environment professionals play a central role in implementing sustainable practices, adoption remains inconsistent across disciplines and sustainability domains.

Existing research has largely focused on policy awareness, green building technologies, or institutional frameworks. Less attention has been given to the structural conditions that enable professionals to translate sustainability knowledge into practice.

This study addresses that gap by examining the factors influencing sustainable practice adoption while identifying priority intervention domains capable of generating the greatest sustainability impact.

---

# Research Objectives

This study seeks to:

- Examine sustainable practice adoption among built environment professionals.
- Assess sustainability policy awareness.
- Evaluate the relationship between infrastructure and sustainable practice adoption.
- Identify factors influencing implementation.
- Compare current professional practices with perceived areas of greatest contribution.
- Develop evidence-based recommendations for sustainable urban development.

# Methodology

## Study Design

- Cross-sectional mixed-methods research
- Survey of 147 built environment professionals

The analysis includes:

- Descriptive statistics
- Pearson correlation analysis
- Multiple linear regression
- Model diagnostics
- Publication-quality visualization
- Conceptual framework development

Statistical analyses were performed in **Python** and independently validated against **SPSS** outputs.

# Key Findings

The study produced five principal findings.

### Infrastructure is the strongest predictor of sustainable practice adoption.

Infrastructure availability consistently emerged as the only statistically significant predictor across multiple regression models.

### Awareness alone does not guarantee implementation.

Although respondents demonstrated relatively high sustainability awareness, awareness variables explained comparatively little variation in adoption after accounting for infrastructure and organisational factors.

### Professionals recognise where they can contribute most.

Respondents consistently identified **Green Infrastructure**, **Climate-responsive Design**, and **Water Conservation** as the sustainability domains with the greatest potential contribution despite comparatively low levels of current implementation.

### The Adoption–Contribution Gap

One of the study's principal contributions is the development of the **Adoption–Contribution Gap**, which compares current implementation with perceived contribution to identify sustainability domains exhibiting the greatest unrealised implementation potential.

### Policy Implications

The findings suggest that future interventions should prioritise strengthening infrastructure, governance, institutional capacity, financing mechanisms, and implementation support rather than relying solely on awareness campaigns.
